Anshu Mishra

I am an undergraduate in mathematics at IISER Berhampur. I am interested in mathematical biology and modelling — how differential equations say something real about living systems.

During undergrad I am trying to get as much exposure as possible to mathematical biology, bioinformatics, and related areas. On the longer term I think I want to work on theoretical ecology or mathematical oncology, though I am not sure yet what that looks like exactly.

I write occasionally at mathematabio.

Reach me at anshumishra1729@gmail.com.

Current work

This summer I am working on delay differential equations of the tumor–immune system with treatment and control — trying to understand what time delays do to the qualitative behaviour of the system.

I am also reading Van Kampen's Stochastic Processes in Physics and Chemistry, with the aim of eventually connecting this to stochastic models in population biology.
